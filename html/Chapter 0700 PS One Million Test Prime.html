>Chapter 0700 PS One Million Test Prime

This is an example of testing the *fam formulas. The top , a midpoint and the bottom views are presented. It is a large view of the all the prime numbers around a million. The *fam is across the top , the prime numbers on the left. And , the solution to the formula PSL =(PS - *fam) / 30 is inside the matrix.

Some middle point.

Look at PS 0726191 and 0726121 , 'just another thirty away'.
The PSL is a delta of one.
The same is true for 0726337 and 0726367 ; with PSL of 24211 and 24212. 'just another thirty away'.

Another middle point.

Look at 0861947 and 0861977 ; then 0862067 and 0862097. 'just another thirty away'.

Close to a million - the end

Look at very bottom. 100003 and 100033. 'just another thirty away'.
Using the *fam formulas and solutions the next prime within a *fam is never less than thirty away.
It could be more , but will be some value times 'just another thirty away'.
Look at 0999667 and 0999727 , the distance is 60. Next , look at the PSL 33322 and 33324 , the distance is 2.

2 x 30 =60 , even though we had to go twice it is still 'just another thirty away'.
The missing value between the two is 0999697.

PSL =(0999697 - *fam07) / 30 =33323. The missing PSL in the chart.
It has factors 1 , 953 , 1049 , 0999697.
